Metrics Explanations

SEO (Search Engine Optimization):

What is it? The practice of improving website visibility in traditional search engine results through technical HTML structure and metadata.

What does it mean? Measures how well your site is optimized for classic Google algorithms.

Good SEO: Unique titles, clear header hierarchy, mobile-optimized design.

Meta Tags:

What is it? HTML elements providing metadata about a webpage (title, description, canonical URLs).

What does it mean? How well page metadata communicates content relevance to search engines.

Good Meta: Unique title under 60 chars, compelling description, proper canonical URLs.

Headers (H1-H6):

What is it? HTML elements defining hierarchical structure and outline of content.

What does it mean? How well content is organized for search engines and users.

Good Headers: One H1 per page, logical hierarchy, accurately describe content.

Mobile:

What is it? How well your site performs on mobile devices including Core Web Vitals.

What does it mean? Whether your site provides fast, accessible mobile experience.

Good Mobile: Responsive design, fast loading, touch-friendly interface.

AEO (Answer Engine Optimization):

What is it? Optimizing content to appear in direct answers, featured snippets, and voice search.

What does it mean? How effectively content answers direct questions for "position zero" results.

Good AEO: Direct, concise answers, proper FAQ/HowTo schema, clear content structure.

Directness:

What is it? How effectively content provides immediate, clear answers without fluff.

What does it mean? Whether content immediately answers queries in concise, extractable form.

Good Directness: Answers at beginning, concise language, structured data for snippets.

Schema:

What is it? Structured data code (Schema.org) helping search engines understand content context.

What does it mean? How well content is marked up with structured data types.

Good Schema: Relevant schema types, complete data fields, valid JSON-LD.

GEO (Generative Engine Optimization):

What is it? Optimizing content for AI-powered search and Large Language Models.

What does it mean? Probability of being cited by AI models like ChatGPT and Claude.

Good GEO: Authoritative content, strong entity signals, structured data for LLMs.

Citability:

What is it? Likelihood your content will be referenced by AI language models.

What does it mean? Whether content has characteristics LLMs look for in sources.

Good Citability: Well-researched content, clear authorship, unique value.

Authority (E-E-A-T):

What is it? Expertise, experience, authoritativeness, and trustworthiness signals.

What does it mean? Whether content demonstrates topical expertise and trust signals.

Good Authority: Demonstrated expertise, credible backlinks, transparent author info.

LLM Citation Share:

What is it? Estimated percentage of AI responses referencing your content.

What does it mean? How often AI systems reference your content in responses.

Good LLM Citation: Higher percentage = more AI visibility, well-structured content.

          Audit Metrics Explained

          What is SEO?

          Search Engine Optimization (SEO) is the practice of improving a website's visibility in traditional search engine results through technical HTML structure and metadata quality.

          What does it mean?

          SEO measures how well your site is optimized for classic Google search algorithms through technical foundations like meta tags, header structure, and mobile responsiveness.

          What constitutes good SEO?

          • Unique, descriptive title and meta description tags
          • Clear H1-H6 header hierarchy with semantic structure
          • Mobile-optimized design with fast loading times

          What is AEO?

          Answer Engine Optimization (AEO) is the practice of optimizing content to appear in direct answer boxes, featured snippets, and voice search results.

          What does it mean?

          AEO measures how effectively your content answers direct questions and powers "position zero" results in search engines through structured, concise information.

          What constitutes good AEO?

          • Direct, concise answers to common questions
          • Proper FAQ and HowTo schema implementation
          • Clear content structure that search engines can parse

          What is GEO?

          Generative Engine Optimization (GEO) is the practice of optimizing content for AI-powered search engines and Large Language Models to appear in AI-generated citations.

          What does it mean?

          GEO measures your brand's probability of being cited by AI models like ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini when they generate responses to user queries.

          What constitutes good GEO?

          • Authoritative, well-cited content with clear sources
          • Strong entity signals and topical expertise
          • Structured data that LLMs can easily parse and reference

          What is Meta?

          Meta tags are HTML elements that provide metadata about a webpage, including title, description, and canonical URLs.

          What does it mean?

          Meta scoring evaluates how well your page's title, description, and other metadata communicate content relevance to search engines.

          What constitutes good Meta?

          • Unique, descriptive title under 60 characters
          • Compelling meta description with target keywords
          • Proper canonical URLs to avoid duplicate content

          What are Headers?

          Headers (H1-H6) are HTML elements that define the hierarchical structure and outline of your webpage content.

          What does it mean?

          Header scoring assesses how well your content is organized into a logical hierarchy that search engines can understand and users can navigate.

          What constitutes good Headers?

          • One clear H1 tag per page with primary keyword
          • Logical H2-H6 hierarchy without skipping levels
          • Headers accurately describe the content that follows

          What is Mobile?

          Mobile optimization refers to how well your website performs and displays on mobile devices, including Core Web Vitals and responsive design.

          What does it mean?

          Mobile scoring evaluates whether your site provides a fast, accessible experience on smartphones and tablets, which is critical since most searches now occur on mobile.

          What constitutes good Mobile?

          • Responsive design that adapts to all screen sizes
          • Fast loading times (Core Web Vitals scores)
          • Touch-friendly buttons and readable text sizes

          What is Directness?

          Directness measures how effectively your content provides immediate, clear answers to user questions without unnecessary fluff or ambiguity.

          What does it mean?

          Directness scoring evaluates whether your content immediately answers the user's query in a concise, authoritative manner that can be extracted for featured snippets.

          What constitutes good Directness?

          • Answers positioned at the beginning of content
          • Clear, concise language without unnecessary jargon
          • Structured data that search engines can parse for snippets

          What is Schema?

          Schema markup (Schema.org) is structured data code that helps search engines understand the context and meaning of your content.

          What does it mean?

          Schema scoring assesses how well your content is marked up with structured data types like FAQ, LocalBusiness, Organization, and Product schemas.

          What constitutes good Schema?

          • Relevant schema types matching your content
          • Complete, accurate data fields within each schema
          • Valid JSON-LD implementation without syntax errors

          What is Citability?

          Citability measures the likelihood that your content will be referenced or cited by AI language models when generating responses.

          What does it mean?

          Citability scoring evaluates whether your content has the characteristics that LLMs look for when selecting sources for AI-generated answers.

          What constitutes good Citability?

          • Well-researched, factually accurate content
          • Clear authorship and publication authority
          • Content that provides unique value or insights

          What is Authority?

          Authority (E-E-A-T) measures the expertise, experience, authoritativeness, and trustworthiness of your content and brand.

          What does it mean?

          Authority scoring evaluates whether your content demonstrates topical expertise and trust signals that search engines and AI models recognize as credible.

          What constitutes good Authority?

          • Demonstrated expertise in your field
          • Credible backlinks and citations from authoritative sources
          • Clear author bios and organizational transparency

          What is LLM Citation Share?

          LLM Citation Share is the estimated percentage of AI-generated responses that reference your content when answering related queries.

          What does it mean?

          This metric uses model probing to estimate how often AI systems like ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini reference your content in their responses.

          What constitutes good LLM Citation Share?

          • Higher percentage = more AI visibility
          • Content that answers common industry questions
          • Well-structured, authoritative information
