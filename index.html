          Metrics Explanations

          SEO (Search Engine Optimization):

          What is it? The practice of improving website visibility in traditional search engine results through technical HTML structure and metadata.

          What does it mean? Measures how well your site is optimized for classic Google algorithms.

          Good SEO: Unique titles, clear header hierarchy, mobile-optimized design.

          Meta Tags:

          What is it? HTML elements providing metadata about a webpage (title, description, canonical URLs).

          What does it mean? How well page metadata communicates content relevance to search engines.

          Good Meta: Unique title under 60 chars, compelling description, proper canonical URLs.

          Headers (H1-H6):

          What is it? HTML elements defining hierarchical structure and outline of content.

          What does it mean? How well content is organized for search engines and users.

          Good Headers: One H1 per page, logical hierarchy, accurately describe content.

          Mobile:

          What is it? How well your site performs on mobile devices including Core Web Vitals.

          What does it mean? Whether your site provides fast, accessible mobile experience.

          Good Mobile: Responsive design, fast loading, touch-friendly interface.

          AEO (Answer Engine Optimization):

          What is it? Optimizing content to appear in direct answers, featured snippets, and voice search.

          What does it mean? How effectively content answers direct questions for "position zero" results.

          Good AEO: Direct, concise answers, proper FAQ/HowTo schema, clear content structure.

          Directness:

          What is it? How effectively content provides immediate, clear answers without fluff.

          What does it mean? Whether content immediately answers queries in concise, extractable form.

          Good Directness: Answers at beginning, concise language, structured data for snippets.

          Schema:

          What is it? Structured data code (Schema.org) helping search engines understand content context.

          What does it mean? How well content is marked up with structured data types.

          Good Schema: Relevant schema types, complete data fields, valid JSON-LD.

          GEO (Generative Engine Optimization):

          What is it? Optimizing content for AI-powered search and Large Language Models.

          What does it mean? Probability of being cited by AI models like ChatGPT and Claude.

          Good GEO: Authoritative content, strong entity signals, structured data for LLMs.

          Citability:

          What is it? Likelihood your content will be referenced by AI language models.

          What does it mean? Whether content has characteristics LLMs look for in sources.

          Good Citability: Well-researched content, clear authorship, unique value.

          Authority (E-E-A-T):

          What is it? Expertise, experience, authoritativeness, and trustworthiness signals.

          What does it mean? Whether content demonstrates topical expertise and trust signals.

          Good Authority: Demonstrated expertise, credible backlinks, transparent author info.

          LLM Citation Share:

          What is it? Estimated percentage of AI responses referencing your content.

          What does it mean? How often AI systems reference your content in responses.

          Good LLM Citation: Higher percentage = more AI visibility, well-structured content.
